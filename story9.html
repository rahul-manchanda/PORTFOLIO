GAIL longest Pipeline Launch with 270-Degree Projection
Objective: Launch India’s longest gas pipeline project in 2012, inaugurated by Hon’ble Prime Minister Dr. Manmohan Singh.
Project Details:
  • The gas is transported to Dahej port from the Middle East via carrier ships.
  • At Dahej, Gujarat, the gas is processed and pressurized for dispatch to Vijay Nagar, Madhya Pradesh.
  • From Vijay Nagar, the gas is further dispatched to its final destination in Bhatinda.
Vision: A gas pipeline brings significant prosperity to the region, essential for industrialization and economic growth. The launch aimed to highlight the transformative impact on the lives of people in Punjab.
Challenge: Due to Prime Minister Manmohan Singh’s poor health and tight schedule, the PMO recommended a remote launch from Delhi.
Day of the Event:
  • The Prime Minister remotely inaugurated the pipeline as planned.
  • The projection begins by illustrating the carrier ship berthing the gas at Dahej Port, Gujarat, followed by its dispatch to Vijay Nagar, Madhya Pradesh.
  • The station master at Vijay Nagar acknowledged receipt of the gas and its dispatch to Bhatinda, post repressuring the gas.
  • The film depicted the prosperity the pipeline would bring as the gas traveled through various regions in between.
  • In the final frame, the gas reached Bhatinda, and the station master expressed gratitude to the Prime Minister.
Result:The event was a tremendous success, garnering positive media coverage. The virtual launch set a new standard for future remote inauguration.
